Frequently Asked Questions

1. General Questions

Volt is a pan-european, progressive movement with the idea to revolutionise the way politics is done by working towards a united, fair and open society that provides equal opportunities for everyone.

Volt was founded after the Brexit vote by Andrea Venzon, who is also the current elected president of Volt. On March 29th, 2017, the day article 50 was triggered, Volt was only a Facebook page with five members questioning the status quo. Without funding, major names or political support, Volt has in the meantime grown to a movement with thousands of supporters in all European countries.

Our initiative was started, because reactionary and populistic tendencies were and are still threatening the values, our members believe in and because traditional political parties seem to not be able to propose attractive, adequate and pragmatic responses to the challenges of our time.

Volt is a transnational movement. Our goal is to create political parties in european countries in order to participate in elections on European, national and local level with shared values, political guidelines and goals across all countries.

Our initial goal was to participate in the Euorpean elections in 2019 in at least seven european countries. Why seven countries? Because if we would have succeeded to win 25 seats across seven european countries, we could have formed the first independent faction in the European Parliament - something which hasn't been done before in the history of the European Union. With the European elections now passed and our first MEP elected in Germany, our focus is now shifting to natoinal and local elections across the continent.

The name "Volt" was derived from the international unit for electric potential. We chose this name because we want to bring new energy to politics and because it also represents our idea of being pragmatic and efficient. In addition, it is a word pronounced in the same way across all countries.

2. Political Position

A united Europe and society which values its citizens and empowers them to reach their individual potential and together strive for the highest standards of human, social, ecological and technical development.

We are at home in our cities and communities. In our regions. In our countries. In Europe. We want to have challenges addressed on the respective, subsidiary level. Speaking for Europe, it is currently not empowered to solve European issues with national governments and their interests deciding on Europe's behalf and its citizens having to bear the consequences.

We advocate a democratic system following the rule of law. We don't want to position us "left" or "right". Rather we would like to find solutions to issues mattering to us and develop policies based on scientific proof and proven political practices.

Volt commits itself to the European Union's core values - human dignity, liberty, democracy, equality, the rule of law and human rights - as well as to fostering a society marked by integration, tolerance, justice, solidarity and the absence of discrimination. As societies we should strive for the highest living standards for all citizens.

3. Financing

Up until now we have been financing ourselves through donations from members, families and friends. We are in the process of setting up membership fees. Aside from this we have successfully ran several crowdfunding campaigns in some countries and are planning to continue funding us through these means in the future.
